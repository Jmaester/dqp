Blog Search

Using the same technology that powers Google's web search, Google Blog Search provides fresh, relevant search results from millions of feed-enabled blogs. Users can search for blog posts, blog names, authors, or a specific date range.

Blogger is Google's web-based publishing tool that gives people the ability to publish to the web instantly in a blog format. Blogs are web pages made up of short, informal, frequently updated posts that are arranged chronologically.

Custom Search Engine

Built with Google's core search technology, Custom Search Engine prioritizes or restricts search results based on websites and pages that users specify, and can be tailored to reflect their point of view or area of expertise. There are features for customizing the look and feel, monetizing through Google AdSense, and collaborating with others to include more sites in their search index.

Gmail

Gmail is Google's free webmail service. It comes with more than 2,600 megabytes (and increasing every day) of free storage and built-in Google search technology. Because of its capacity and search capabilities, users don't need to stay ahead of webmail storage limitations, unearth messages buried in folders, or worry about taming an unruly inbox. Gmail users can keep all of their important messages, files and pictures in a single place, and search them quickly and easily to find whatever they're looking for. Gmail also offers a new way of viewing messages as part of conversations. And Gmail's powerful spam filtering eliminates spam from one's daily routine. Gmail is currently available in 38 different languages, and offers virus protection, customizable from-addresses, and free automatic forwarding and POP access. It is also accessible for free on web-enabled mobile phones and devices. Gmail is in beta and is available by invitation or by receiving an invitation code to your mobile phone.

Google Calendar

Google Calendar is a free web-based calendar service that makes it easier to keep all the calendar information important to you in one place. Google Calendar also enables you to share your own calendar information, whether you want to send party invitations to friends, or if you want to publicize events from your website. And Google Calendar uses open standards like iCal and XML, so it's compatible with many other calendar formats.

Google Desktop

Google Desktop is a free client application that allows you to easily search and personalize your computer. It provides full text search over email, files, music, photos, chats, web pages that you've viewed, and more. By making your computers searchable, Google Desktop puts your information easily within your reach and frees you from having to manually organize your files. Google Desktop also helps you gather new information from the web with Sidebar and Google Gadgets, visually appealing mini-applications that can display anything from your new email to the local weather forecast to personalized news. With Google Desktop, you get personalized information when you want it, right on your desktop.

Google Earth

Google Earth delivers a 3D digital model of the entire earth via the Internet. The groundbreaking EarthStream™ technology combines advanced 3D graphics and network streaming innovations in a high performance system that runs on standard PCs and commodity servers. Users can point and zoom to anyplace on the planet. Satellite images zoom into view, enhanced by Google search to show local points of interest and facts.

Google Finance

Google Finance (beta) offers a broad range of information about stocks, mutual funds and public and private companies along with charts, news and fundamental financial data. Users can search for stocks and funds by name; plot market data with corresponding news stories; participate in discussion groups; learn about company management; keep track of their portfolios; and read relevant blog posts from across the web.

Google Groups

Google Groups enables easy participation in Internet discussion groups by providing users with tools to search, read and browse these groups and to post messages of their own. Users can quickly create their own announcement lists, mailing lists and public discussions. Every Google Group has its own Google-fast search, which makes it easy to find discussions deep in a group's archive.

Google Toolbar

Google Toolbar is a free browser tool that makes web browsing more efficient and effective. With the Toolbar, users can access Google search from any website, get instant search suggestions as they type, and even specify custom searches. In addition to search tools, users can share web pages via email, blog, or SMS, add buttons and bookmarks, translate words, check spelling on web forms, block pop-up ads, and much more. Google Toolbar is available for Internet Explorer and for Firefox on the Windows, Macintosh and Linux platforms.

Personalized Search

Personalized Search orders Google search results based on what is most relevant to an individual user. The Search History feature of Personalized Search enables users to view and manage their history of searches and which results they've clicked on. Over time, this information personalizes future searches by bringing results closer to the top when it's clear they are the most relevant.

Picasa & Picasa Web Albums

Picasa and Picasa Web Albums are two independent products that work together to help you enjoy and share your digital photos. Picasa is free downloadable software for Windows and Linux that helps users instantly organize, edit and be creative with all the photos on their PC. Picasa also makes sharing your favorite photos exceptionally simple -- with the click of a button, users can upload (and even sync) their photos to Picasa Web Albums, Google's free online photo-sharing service. Picasa Web Albums makes photo sharing fun with features like drag-and-drop mapping, beautiful full-screen slideshows, the ability to automatically help organize and share photos based on who's in them, mobile access, and more.
