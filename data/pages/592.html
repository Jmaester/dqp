How to search

  1. Visit images.google.com , type what you want to see in the search box, and click Search Images. Google will search the web for all types of images that seem relevant to your search.
  2. Hover your cursor over an image to learn more about it.
  3. Click an image to see a larger version, additional information, and the webpage where the image is from.

Overview of the page

Here's a quick guide to the elements that you'll find on a Google Images search results page.

Header & search bar

Google products bar
See links for various Google services and a "more" menu for additional services that aren't listed. Use these links to navigate to basic web search, Gmail, and other Google products .

Settings
Click this link to access your Search settings . If you're signed in, you'll also be able to see your Google Account settings (like your sign-in email address).

Sign in & out
Sign in to a Google Account to personalize your search experience. If you're signed in to your Google Account, the email address associated with your account will be shown on the top of the page:

    example@google.com | Settings ▼ | Sign out  
If you share a computer and don't want others to have access to your Google Account, be sure to always click Sign out when you're finished using the computer.

Search bar
To do a search on Google Images, just type in a few descriptive search terms, then hit Enter on your keyboard or click the Search button.

Advanced search
For more precise searches, click the link to visit the Advanced Image Search page where you can enter more details about what you're looking for.

SafeSearch
You'll see a link next to the search bar that lets you change your SafeSearch settings. The link will open a menu with several options. To control the likeliness that you'll see explicit or adult content, change your filtering setting to Off, Moderate, or Strict. Learn about SafeSearch settings.

Search statistics
See the total number of results and how long the search took to complete.

Search results

Once you enter a search, you'll see pages of image thumbnails that seem relevant to what you're looking for.

Pages of results
The images are arranged into pages, and you can use the scroll bar to browse downwards through the pages. If your search returns many related images, we'll typically show you the first few hundred images. To see additional images, scroll down to the very bottom of the page and click Show more results. Images are arranged according to their relevance to your search as well as their dimensions.

Image details
Hover your cursor over an image to see the hover-over pane with details about the image. Click an image to go to the webpage where the image came from.

Related searches
For some searches, you'll see some links to related searches shown above the image results. These searches are commonly associated with your original search, so we think you might find them relevant. Click one of these links to see image search results for that topic.

Sponsored links
When you search Google, you'll sometimes see ads shown above your image results. The ads are relevant to your search and can provide you with valuable information about what you're looking for. If there are no relevant ads to show with your search, then we won't you show any. Ad revenues help us bring you free products such as Search, Gmail, Google Maps, Google Docs, and more. If you're interested in advertising your website on Google, learn about the Google AdWords program .

Image details

  • Name: The first line of a result shows the image's file name. The name is also a link, so you can click it to reach the webpage that shows the image.
  • Size: The width and height of the image in pixels. For reference, a good size for a picture on a website is about 400 x 300, a typical computer screen is at least 1024 x 768, and a 12 megapixel photo from a digital camera is about 4000 x 3000 pixels.
  • Description: This brief snippet can come from one of several places on the image's webpage including the image's "alt" tag, caption text, or other content on the page.
  • URL: In green text, you'll see the webpage where the image is from.

See other images

  • Similar: See other images that are similar in appearance or meaning to your selected image.

Tools & filters

The panel along the side of your results page will dynamically show the tools and filters that we think are most useful for your particular search. Use these links to filter and customize the results you see.
  • Type of content: If you decide that you'd like to see other types of results besides images, use the links along the left. You can switch to see results of a particular type like News or Discussions (click More to see a full list), or to see all types of content, click Everything.

  • Recommended tools: Limit the image results you see by using the tools and filters shown on the left. For example, see only images of a certain type like just photographs or line drawings, or filter your results by image size or predominant color.

Learn more about these search options.

Bottom of the page

If you scroll down to the very bottom of the page, you'll see some helpful links and resources including Help and the Images homepage .

More results
If the image you're looking for isn't shown, you can click the Show more results button at the bottom of the page.

Social search links
These links provide more information about your social search results. Click My social circle to see the network of connections that Google uses to identify relevant social search results. Click My social content to see a list of your content that might be shown to people in your social circle. You'll only see these links if you are signed in to your Google Account and meet other requirements (like having a social circle that includes people with Flickr or Picasa Web photos associated with their accounts).

Feedback link
Unhappy with your Google results for a particular search? Have a great idea to improve image search? Want to share a Google Images experience you've had? Click the Give us feedback link to share your thoughts with us.
