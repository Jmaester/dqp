What is Report a problem?
One of the exciting things about maps and geography is that the world is a constantly changing place, and keeping on top of these changes is a never-ending endeavor. It's the sort of challenge we like to take on here at Google, and we want to give you the opportunity to help with a new tool on Google Maps. After all, the best experts on a place are the people that live there. Report a problem enables you to tell us about changes in the world around you that aren't reflected in our maps. It's also a tool for letting us know of any edits or suggestions you have for our maps. 

Has that new highway on-ramp finally opened up? Do we have an outdated name for your local school? Was Main Street converted into a pedestrian-only walkway? This tool enables you to tell us about it. For now this is only available in a limited number of areas, but we're working to expand as quickly as possible.

How to access and use Report a problem:
1) You can access Report a Problem in a couple of places:  
  • Right click on the map and select "Report a Problem" 
  • Click the "Report a Problem" link at the bottom right of the map
  • Click the "Report a Problem" link below driving directions
  • Click the "Report a Problem" link below the search results on Maps
2) Tell us about your suggestion or problem with Google Maps. Some examples include:
  • Search results - let us know if we're missing your favorite restaurant or are missing a new development in your town.
  • Driving Directions - if you know a better route that the locals use, we want to include that as well.
  • General map issues - if streets are missing or roads are renamed, let us know so we can show them accurately. 
3) Click submit to send us your suggestion or problem. When you opt in to get email updates, you'll typically get no more than three emails from us:

  • We'll let you know that we've received the issue and give you the Issue ID.
  • Once we've reviewed the issue, we'll let you know what we decided to do (such as fix it).
  • If we make a change you can see on Google Maps, we'll tell you that it's been implemented. This email also includes instructions on how to get in touch with us if you don't think the change is correct.
If you aren't interested in receiving email updates on an issue, make sure to uncheck the "Email me when the problem is resolved" option.

This just gives you an idea of the sorts of things you can let us know about. If you can't find an option that describes the issue you have encountered, there is a general category that you can use to tell us about other map issues.

What is the review and correction process?
Once we've received your edit or suggestion we'll confirm it with other users, data providers, or Street View imagery. If you submit your email address when you submitted your report, we'll keep you posted on the progress of your suggestion or edit. We hope to address and resolve each edit within a month.

In some cases we might not be able to immediately confirm a solution to your problem. Please be patient. As more people tell us about the issue, we'll have more information that we can use to verify the fix we need to make. Remember, if you sign in and let us know you want to be kept updated, we'll keep you posted on any changes to the status of your issue.

What if I make an error during my submission?
As we noted in the review and correction process, we confirm all edits and suggestions against a variety of sources, so your report will not cause any changes to the map data if we cannot verify that it's valid. In addition, you can resubmit a new report and reference your original Problem ID to ensure we take the appropriate action.

If you have map data you would like displayed to millions of users around the world in Google Maps (such as homebuilders, state and local governments, or tourist boards), please see our Map Content Provider partner site for more information on how to work with Google.
