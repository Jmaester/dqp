Fix an error on Google Maps

Help us make Maps better

Community edits allow you to modify the information you see on Google Maps, making it more accurate for everyone.

Find out how you can:

Report incorrect map data if you found an error regarding a street, address, driving directions, or search result.

If you notice that the underlying map data is incorrect, you can report the error to the data provider. Our data providers are listed in the lower right-hand corner of the map. In some cases, you may have to zoom in to see the specific provider for an area.

How to report errors to specific data providers:
  • Google: Use the Report a Problem link. This is currently available in Australia, Austria, Belgium, Canada, Denmark, Lichtenstein, Netherlands, New Zealand, Norway, South Africa, Switzerland, and the United States. In areas where Map Maker is available, you can edit map data directly.
  • Tele Atlas: Report a map error using their Map Insight system.
Other providers will be added as tools become available for users to submit corrections. If you don't find your local data provider in the above list and Map Maker isn't available, please locate the correct data provider on the lower right-hand corner of the map and contact them directly.

Add a point of interest that you think your neighbors would want to find.

If you are a business owner, you can add your business to Google Places. You can also verify an existing listing and add additional details about your business.

To let us know about a point of interest other than a business, use the Report a Problem link and follow these instructions:

  1. Drag the marker to the position where the point of interest is located.
  2. Under "Which element is wrong?", select "A place or landmark."
  3. Under "Which place?", select "Place isn't listed."
  4. Fill out the details under "Problem Description" and click Report Problem

Move the marker for a location if it's in the wrong place.

If you notice that a marker is positioned incorrectly, you can move it to the right spot on the map.

  1. Optional: Switch to Satellite view. This allows you to move the marker more accurately, as you can see both roads and satellite imagery.
  2. After you search for a place, click the marker you would like to re-position.The info window will appear.
  3. To make changes, click Edit.
  4. Click Move marker and drag the marker to the correct location. Use the X at the base of the marker to help you place it correctly. We recommend placing the marker at the entrance of the location, as this helps improve the accuracy of our driving directions.
  5. Click Save.

Notes:

  • If you move the marker more than 200 meters away from its original location, we'll need to review this change before it can appear in Google Maps.
  • If you can't edit a business location, it may be because the business owner has already verified the business on Google Places. Verified listings can only be edited by the owner. If a listed business is yours, be sure to claim your business.
  • If you do not see an "Edit" link in the info window for a location, you can use the Report a problem link to let us know about the error.

Edit details for a local business that you notice are incorrect.

If you are a business owner and have already claimed and verified your listing, follow these instructions to edit your listing.

You can edit details such as the business name, address, phone, category, or website:

  1. Once you've located your business on Google Maps, select the business's marker. The info window will appear.
  2. To make changes, click more and select Edit details.
  3. Make the appropriate changes and click Publish.

You can also edit this information directly from your Place Page. Click Edit this place at the top of the Place Page to edit the business listing.

If you are the owner of this business, feel free to use community edits to fix your listing, then claim your business. Businesses whose listings have been claimed and verified through Google Places can't be edited by the community or flagged for removal. Claiming your listing through Google Places is the only way to prevent community edits to your listing.

Report a listing on Google Maps that's closed down, a duplicate of another listing, or contains private information.

If you see a place on the map that isn't there anymore (or perhaps was never there in the first place), let us know about it. Here's how:

  1. After you search for a place, click the appropriate marker. The info window appears.
  2. Click Edit > Report a problem.
  3. Select the issue that pertains to this place and add any comments you have.
  4. Click Report a problem. Google will investigate the place you have reported.

Users cannot report problems for owner-verified business listings on Google Places. Visit Google Places to find out how to verify or remove your business listing.

Track your edits.

To view a history of the edits made to a particular place, open the info window and click Edit. Click View history in the info window to see a list of edits. To see only the edits you've made, click My Profile at the top right of your screen.

Other ways to edit

Google Map Maker allows you to contribute, share and edit map information for 174 countries and territories. Map Maker provides a set of tools to create and edit map features in order to improve the quality of maps and local information in your region. Learn how to get started with Map Maker.

Go beyond edits

Community edits, such as adjusting marker locations, will appear in Google Maps search results. You can also create a custom map that highlights specific locations, areas, and routes and share it with others using My Maps. Points of interest that you create on your custom map won't appear in Google Maps search results.

To access this feature, click the My Maps tab in Google Maps. Or, learn more about custom maps.
