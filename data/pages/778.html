Advertiser

Digg Ads is a way for advertisers to provide sponsored content to users. While it looks and feels like an organic news items, Digg Ads are created by advertisers and direct users to an advertiser’s content.

For the Advertiser

  • Advertisers can create their own ad by entering a headline, URL, display URL, submitting a 1×1” thumbnail image and summary description that is viewable on the story item
  • Each ad has a “Sponsored by [Company name]” tag and appears as a story-item within the storylist
  • Advertiser competes in an auction which is a function of their cost-per-click bids and the number of Diggs
  • The ads must directly relate to the content on the landing page and must clearly represent the company, product, or brand that is being advertised.

For the user

  • A user can digg an ad or close an ad similar to a story item
  • If a user doesn’t like a Digg Ads, they can remove the story from their view by clicking on the ‘x’ that appears in the top right of the story item when their mouse hovers on the story. This will remove the story from all views and ensure that it doesn’t appear again for that user
  • A user can click on the headline to go to a landing page that is hosted by the advertiser
  • Once on a landing page, a user can return to Digg by selecting the back button
