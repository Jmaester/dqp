Digg Dialogg with Marissa Mayer of Google

<< Back to Blog
Submitted by Jen Burton on October 20, 2010 - 2:48pm
  • Company

Hey Everyone,

We’re excited to announce that our next Digg Dialogg is here. This time around, we’re bringing back one of our favorite Dialogg guests, technology luminary Marissa Mayer, for an insightful look into the past, present and future of Google. Considering her recent change in role to Vice President, Location & Local Services of Google and the launch of Sony Internet TV powered by Google TV, we think you'll agree that this will be a great discussion.

From now until Monday, October 25th at 12:00pm PDT, submit your questions as a comment on this blog post and then Digg those you’d like to see answered by Marissa. The interview will be conducted from a Sony Internet TV outfitted room, so look forward to seeing a few live action demos of how this product actually works.

In case you're new to Digg Dialogg, here's how it works: You have the opportunity to submit and Digg questions that we then pose to influential leaders and luminaries. Submit and vote on your favorite questions on this post from now until Monday, October 25th at Noon PDT; we'll be posting the full interview on Monday, November 1st at Noon PDT.

Update: video will be posted Tuesday, November 2 at Noon PDT

Thanks,

Jen

Bio on Marissa: Marissa joined Google back in 1999 as the first female engineer and 20th company employee. As Vice President of Search Products and User Experience, she has been responsible for leading and developing products including Google Search, Gmail, Google Maps, Google Earth and iGoogle. Earlier this month, Marissa was named to Google’s Operating Committee and placed in charge of all geographic and local services.
