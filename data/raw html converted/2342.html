Some changes to Diggable ads on Digg

<< Back to Blog
Submitted by Jen Burton on October 20, 2010 - 9:30am
  • Company

We launched “Digg Ads” over a year ago – these advertisements are content-based and allow the Digg community to Digg or hide (buries will be back very soon) the advertisement just as they would any other story item on the site.

One change we’re making is that we’re going to try out Digg and hide buttons on some of the display ads across the site. Usually you’ll see these display ads in the upper right corner of the site.

When you Digg any ad (display or story-format), the story attached converts to a regular Digg story. That news story is just like any other — you can Digg, hide, or write a comment.

In addition to this, we’re testing Digg Ad sharing by automatically sharing any ad you Digg with friends who follow your My News feed. If you support the cause of a given company, or just like their creativity, you can easily share the content with your friends.

We all know that advertising is how Digg makes money. Our goal with these changes is to create an advertising model that works for both our advertisers and our community. By collecting your feedback (in the form of Diggs and hides on the ads), we’re sending a signal to our advertisers to encourage them to create content that resonates with our community.

These changes are still in the testing phase, so feel free to let us know what you think.

Thanks,
