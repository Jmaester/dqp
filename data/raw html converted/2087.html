The Quick Answer

When messages go missing in a Gmail account, it's typically due to one of the following:

  • Settings applied to incoming mail, such as a filter or forwarding
  • A configuration issue with IMAP or POP access
  • Deliberate or accidental human action, or possibly a compromised account

To help you investigate what happened to your messages, we'll walk you through a step-by-step process to eliminate possible causes of your missing messages. Let's start by searching for the messages in your account.

To search all messages:

  1. Sign in to Gmail, then click here to prepare a search.
  2. Fill in the search fields with the details you remember confidently. If you're not certain about any field, leave it blank.
  3. In the 'Search:' drop-down menu, select Mail & Spam & Trash.
  4. Click the Search Mail button.
  5. Look for a message in the search results displayed.

Did you find the messages?

Great! We're glad you were able to find your messages. If you're unsure how they ended up where they did, consider the following:

  • Are you accessing Gmail through a mail client or mobile device (e.g., Outlook Express or Apple Mail)?

    • Did you configure your POP Download settings to keep Gmail's copy of messages?
      Make sure you haven't configured your settings to 'delete Gmail's copy,' or Gmail will move all messages to Trash when they're accessed with POP. To keep all messages in your inbox, select keep Gmail's copy in the Inbox from the POP Download drop-down menu.

    • Do you have any filters in your mail client?
      Open your mail client and check for any filters or rules that would affect the messages in question.

    • Are you using recent mode with POP?
      If you use 'recent mode,' configure your mail client to leave messages on the server.

    • Do you use IMAP access?
      If you access Gmail using IMAP,       learn more about how actions sync between Gmail and your mail client or mobile device.

  • Did you delete the missing messages from a label or view?
    Deleting mail removes the conversation from all views in Gmail (Inbox, Sent Mail, All Mail, etc.).

  • Have you configured any features that could affect the messages in question?
    Make sure you haven't created any filters that would affect the messages in question. Also, verify that you haven't enabled forwarding, and that your 'Reply-to:' address is defined as your Gmail address.

  • If you're using a custom 'Send mail as:' address, make sure you've designated your Gmail address as the 'Reply-to:' address.
    If you're sending mail to a mailing list that you subscribe to, your messages will only appear in Sent Mail. This behavior also occurs when sending to an email address that automatically forwards mail back to your Gmail address.

You may need to search with different terms to be sure you haven't missed the messages. Start with fewer details and be sure to look beyond the first page of results.

Also, note that Gmail search doesn't allow partial matches and won't return your result if a word is misspelled or if your search terms are too specific (i.e. a word in the 'Subject:' search field matches but the address you've put in the 'From:' field does not), so we strongly recommend searching with only the information you're sure of.

Still can't find them? Are these messages that you've seen before in your account?

Deleting mail removes a message from all views in Gmail (Inbox, Sent Mail, All Mail, Spam, etc.).

Have you recently deleted any other messages from Sent Mail, or from any label views?

If you haven't emptied Trash, and you deleted these messages less than 30 days ago, you may be able to recover your messages by opening Trash and moving your mail back to your inbox.

Were you able to find and restore your messages from Trash?

Great! We're glad you were able to resolve the problem.

Once a message has been permanently deleted, it can't be recovered. We're sorry for any inconvenience.

Please verify that you are still signed into Gmail, and click here to visit your 'Forwarding and POP/IMAP' settings.

Is POP or IMAP enabled for your account?

  • Do you use POP access?
    • Make sure you haven't configured your settings to delete Gmail's copy, or Gmail will move all messages to Trash when they're accessed with POP. To keep all messages in your inbox, select Keep Gmail's copy in the Inbox from the 'POP Download:' drop-down menu.

    • If you are using 'recent mode' with POP access, configure your POP client to leave messages on the server.

  • Do you use IMAP access?
    • If you access your mail using IMAP, learn more about how actions sync between Gmail and your mail client or mobile device. Make sure you're using our Recommended IMAP client settings.

  • No idea what we're talking about?
    • POP and IMAP are protocols that let you download your mail to your computer, such as to Microsoft Outlook, Apple Mail, or a mobile device. If you're not using an email client to read your mail, try disabling POP and IMAP.

Did this information help?

Great! We're glad you were able to resolve the problem.

Referring again to your 'Forwarding and POP/IMAP' settings, is forwarding enabled?

If so, make sure you haven't configured your settings to 'delete Gmail's copy,' or Gmail will move all messages to Trash when they're forwarded to your other account. To keep all messages in your inbox, select keep Gmail's copy in the Inbox from the drop-down menu.

Filters can automatically move incoming mail to other places in your account. Please check the 'Filters' tab of your Settings, even if you've never configured any. Tip: you can use your browser's find functionality to look for filters that 'Delete' your mail or cause messages to 'Skip Inbox.'

Did you find any filters that delete mail or cause it to skip the inbox?

Please delete the filters that are causing the problem.

Depending on how many messages are missing, we can troubleshoot your problem in different ways.

Approximately how many messages are missing?

In your Gmail inbox, scroll to the bottom of the screen. Right below your storage quota information, you'll find your last account activity. Click Details.

In the new window that opens, check for unusual activity on your account. Unusual activity includes:

  • Activity from a time when you weren't online
  • Activity from a suspicious IP address
  • POP, IMAP, or mobile activity, if you don't use POP, IMAP, or a mobile phone to access Gmail

Note: if you access Gmail from a mobile device, you may see IP addresses from different locations.

Is there any unusual activity on your account?

If you can't find your messages in All Mail, Spam, or Trash, or by performing a search, then they've been permanently removed from Gmail, possibly deleted by someone else. We regret that we are unable to recover messages that have been permanently deleted. If you're concerned that someone may have gained access to your address, we suggest that you take the following measures to protect your account.

In order for us to investigate your issue, please file a report.

To help us investigate, please ask the sender or another recipient for the original copy of the missing message, along with the full message headers.

You can direct the sender or other recipient to http://mail.google.com/support/answer.py?answer=22454 for instructions.

Also, please ask this person to save the headers as a .txt file, using the 'Save As...' option in the Internet Explorer or Safari browser menu, or the 'Save Page As...' option in the Firefox browser menu. It's important that the information is saved this way, rather than copying and pasting.

This person should then forward the file to you as an attachment.
